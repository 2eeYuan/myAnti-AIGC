### Legitimate Academic Transitions (KEEP — Do Not Remove)

These are standard academic writing when followed by citations or data:

- Notably, ...
- Importantly, ...
- Interestingly, ...
- Furthermore, ... / Moreover, ...
- In contrast, ... / Conversely, ...
- Nevertheless, ... / Nonetheless, ...
- Accordingly, ...
- Specifically, ...
- Prior studies have shown that ...
- Previous research has demonstrated that ...
- It has been reported that ...
- Evidence suggests that ...
- Several studies have reported ...

**Rule of thumb:** If followed by a specific citation, data, or concrete finding, it is legitimate. Only flag when vague and unsupported.

## VI. Hedging Replacements

### Reduce Over-Hedging (4-5 layers → 1-2 layers)

| Over-Hedged | Simplified |
|------------|-----------|
| may suggest that X have the potential to confer beneficial effects | suggest that X may reduce |
| it is possible that X could potentially contribute to | X may contribute to |
| appears to be indicative of | suggests, indicates |
| in select patient populations | in some patients |
| have the potential to | may, can |

### Restore Hedging When Too Assertive

| Too Assertive | Appropriately Hedged |
|--------------|---------------------|
| X reduces Y | X may reduce Y (observational) |
| X prevents Y | X may help prevent Y |
| These findings demonstrate | These findings suggest |
| X causes Y | X is associated with Y |

## VIII. Sentence Structure Replacements

### Abstract Noun Subjects → Concrete Subjects

| AI Pattern | Human Pattern |
|-----------|--------------|
| This finding suggests that... | The 40% reduction in X indicates... |
| The results demonstrate that... | Group A showed 15% improvement... |
| This analysis reveals that... | Among 500 patients, 23% experienced... |
| The evidence indicates that... | [Name the specific evidence] |

### Nominalizations → Verbs

| Nominalized | Verbal Form |
|------------|------------|
| the examination of | examining |
| the occurrence of | when X occurs |
| the implementation of | implementing, when X was implemented |
| the utilization of | using |
| the characterization of | characterizing |
| the determination of | determining |

### Repeated Sentence Starters → Varied

If 2+ consecutive sentences start with "This/These/The results/The analysis":

- Use the concrete finding as subject: "The 40% reduction..."
- Use a participant group: "Among older adults..."
- Use a conditional: "When X was present..."
- Use an inverted structure: "What the data do not show..."

## X. Em Dash Replacement Rules

**ZERO TOLERANCE.** Replace every em dash (—).

| Context | Replacement |
|---------|------------|
| Parenthetical/appositive | Commas: "X, a type of Y, does Z" |
| Explanatory aside | Parentheses: "the benefit (a 35% reduction) was significant" |
| Clause break | Period or semicolon: "X occurred. Y followed" |
| Compound modifier | "of" phrase: "fatigue–sleepiness cycle" → "cycle of fatigue and sleepiness" |

**Verification:** After all edits, search for "—" in the output. If any remain, replace them.
